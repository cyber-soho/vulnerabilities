Managing Vulnerabilities: Lab 4

Mapping a Specific CVE to the MITRE ATT&CK Matrix (Part 2)

Welcome to the culmination of the vulnerability management series. In this session, you will extend your ATT&CK mapping from Lab 3 into a comprehensive threat-informed assessment. You will integrate the NIST CSF for governance, D3FEND for countermeasures, and real-world threat intelligence.

Session Planner (3 Hours Max)

Exercise 2 carries double the weight of Exercise 1.

Exercise 1 • 60 Minutes

Cross-Framework Integration

Connect your technical ATT&CK findings to broader governance (NIST CSF) and specific countermeasures (D3FEND). This creates a "full-stack" view of the vulnerability.

Trigger

1. The Vulnerability

The flaw in the system (CVE).

➜
Adversary View

2. MITRE ATT&CK

How they exploit it (Tactics & Techniques).

➜
Countermeasure

3. MITRE D3FEND

Specific technical defense (e.g., File Encryption).

➜
Governance

4. NIST CSF 2.0

Organizational Function (Identify, Protect, Detect).

Execution Steps

1

Map to NIST CSF Functions

Take your ATT&CK mappings and categorize them into NIST Functions (Protect, Detect, etc.).

Hint: "Initial Access" usually maps to Protect (Preventing access) and Detect (Identifying the attempt).
2

Identify D3FEND Countermeasures

Use the D3FEND Matrix Explorer. Search for your mapped offensive technique to find specific defensive technologies.

3

Synthesize Summary

Write a short paragraph explaining the chain from CVE to Governance. This proves strategic understanding.

Exercise 2 • 120 Minutes

Threat-Informed Assessment

Move beyond technical scores. Prioritize action based on real-world adversary behavior and threat intelligence.

Step 1: Semi-Automated Tools

Explore one of the following tools to assist your mapping. Report on how it could have helped in Lab 3.

CISA Decider

Question-driven mapping guide.
TRAM

NLP for threat reports.

Step 2: Intelligence Enrichment

Research these three key factors to inform your prioritization:

  • 1 Active Exploitation: Is it in the CISA KEV catalog?
  • 2 Threat Groups: Are APTs using this? (Check ATT&CK Groups)
  • 3 Sector Relevance: Does this affect critical sectors (Finance, Govt)?
